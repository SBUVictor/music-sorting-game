Module 1: The Foundation

Line 2: Whole Number Sequencing

The first point of sorting after the Class (M, ML, MT) is the whole number.

The Mathematical Principle: Call numbers are sorted by their full numerical value.

Example: M 14.5 represents a value smaller than M 15. On the shelf, 14.5 must sit to the LEFT of 15.

Module 2: Decimal Precision

Line 3: The Cutter Line

The alphanumeric line (e.g., .C36) is the most frequent source of shelving errors.

The Decimal Rule: The number following the letter is always a decimal.

Think of .C4 as .400 and .C36 as .360. Because .36 is smaller than .40, .C36 files before .C4.

Module 3: Complex Sorting

Years, Fascicles, and Series

When the main call number is identical, we sort by sub-units.

The Order of Operations:
  1. Year: 1998 comes before 2005.
  2. Alphabetical: FASC. (Fascicle) comes before SER. (Series).
  3. Numerical: SER. I comes before SER. VI.

Module 4: Special Designations

Thematic Catalog Numbers

Many composers have their own specialized catalog designations.

Key Catalog Labels:
  • KV: Mozart (Koechel)
  • BWV: Bach
  • Hob.: Haydn
  • WoO: Werke ohne Opuszahl (Works without opus). This is a General Label used across many composers for unnumbered works.
